OpsPilot — Privacy Policy

Last updated: 8 June 2026

OpsPilot is an independent, self-contained mobile client for DevOps tools (currently Jenkins and GitLab). It has no backend, no cloud account, and no analytics.

What we collect

Nothing is collected or transmitted to us or any third party. OpsPilot has no servers of its own.

What is stored on your device

  • Connection details you enter (name, URL, username) — stored locally.
  • Credentials (API tokens / passwords / OAuth tokens / reverse-proxy credentials) — stored in the platform secure enclave (Android Keystore).
  • Cached data (jobs, builds, repos, preferences) — in a local database encrypted at rest with AES-256; the key lives in the secure enclave.

All of this stays on your device. Uninstalling the app removes it.

Network access

OpsPilot connects only to the DevOps server(s) you configure (your Jenkins / GitLab), to display and control your builds and repositories. It contacts no other endpoints.

Permissions

  • Internet / network state — to reach your configured servers.
  • Notifications (optional) — to alert you when a build finishes.
  • Biometric (optional) — to lock the app behind your device biometric.

Children

OpsPilot is a developer tool and is not directed at children.
